Proposition 2 is sales tax initiative which funds economic development in Ada, Oklahoma. It was started in 1998 has been sucessfully renewed by voters 3 times since then. The vote to renew the initiative for a 4th time will come this January. We are asking Ada citizens to 'YES' to continue our sucess, invest in our community, and help our economy grow.

Here are examples of some of the projects funded by Proposition 2

Chickasaw Business and Conference Center

The Chickasaw Nation, ECU, and the City of Ada all contributed funds to build the Conference Center, which also houses the ECU Stonecipher School of Business.

Legal Shield

Proposition 2 funds helped retain one of our largest private employers by securing a site for their new headquarter building

GAMI/Tornado Alley

Prop 2 funds helped GAMI construct an additional hanger at the Ada Municipal Airport.

Superior Fabrications

Superior Fabrications expanded into an old Remington plant. Prop 2 funds helped secure the former plant and facilitate the land needed for SFI's expansion.

Globe Manufacturing

Prop 2 funds allowed the Ada Jobs Foundation and Ada Industrial Development Corporation purchase an abandoned Wrangler plant. This plant was sold to Globe Manufacturing.

Flex-N-Gate Incentive

Prop 2 helped incentivize Flex-N-Gate to add jobs and reinvest in the Ada plant facility.

Pontotoc Technology Center Business Incubator Floor

The Technology Center's Business Incubator facility needed a new floor in order to meet FDA regulations for food production.

Oka' Institute

Prop 2 funds matched contributions from the Chickasaw Nation and East Central University in order to launch an institute focused on local water research, policy, and technology.

Council on Law Enforcement Education and Training Facility

Prop 2 helped fund the CLEET facility.

Stone Creek Development

Stone Creek is a new retail development. Prop 2 funds helped the developer construct a new lift station to match increased demand from the new development.

True Steel Incentive

True Steel reloacted in Ada in 2007. Prop 2 funds helped provide incentives for adding quality jobs to the workforce.

Air Evac Incentive

Air Evac recently expanded and added a helicopter facility at the Ada Municipal Airport.

Sykes Building

Prop 2 funds paid for the community-owned call center building, and helped provide incentives for increases in employment.

IRT Incentive

Prop 2 funds paid for the community-owned call center building, and helped provide incentives for increases in employment.

iQor Jobs Incentive

Prop 2 funds paid for the community-owned call center building, and helped provide incentives for increases in employment.

Outcomes and Data

The average wage for Ponotoc County has grown steadily since Proposition 2 first went into effect in 1998. Using dollars adjusted for inflation, the average wage for the county was stagnant in the 1990's. Since Prop 2, the average wage in the county has grown by 30%.
