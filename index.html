Vote 'Yes' on January 10th to Continue Ada's Progress.

Proposition 2 is a quarter of a cent sales tax initiative which funds economic development in Ada, Oklahoma. The election to renew Proposition 2 is will extend our current quarter cent sales tax. It is neither a new tax nor a tax increase. This initiative has been successfully extended by voters 3 times, and the vote to extend the initiative for a 4th time will come this January 10th. A vote 'YES' will help continue our success, invest in the community, and prepare our economy for the future.

Proposition 2 helps fund economic development efforts throughout Ada. Ada has a local organization to support and organize economic development called the Ada Jobs Foundation. The Jobs Foundation supports efforts in business retention and expansion, business attraction, entrepreneurship, and community development. Proposition 2 also funds retail attraction efforts. The Ada Jobs Foundation is recognized as the smallest Accredited Economic Development Organization by the International Economic Development Council, an achievement given to only 53 organizations in North America.

Here are examples of some of the projects funded by Proposition 2

Legal Shield

Proposition 2 funds helped secure a site for a new headquarter building in 2000. Legal Shield has since grown into one of Ada's largest private employers. Legal Shield was founded in Ada and the company stands as an example of Ada's long history of supporting local entrepreneurs.

Flex-N-Gate

Prop 2 provided an incentive to Flex-N-Gate to add jobs at the Ada Plant. Since the incentive, Flex-N-Gate has made a substantial reinvestment in the Ada plant and has added many new jobs. They are now the largest manufacturing employer in Pontotoc County.

Chickasaw Business and Conference Center

Proposition 2 funds helped contribute to building the conference center which also houses ECU's Harland C. Stonecipher School of Business. The center on the ECU campus was funded in large part by the Chickasaw Nation and is another example of the innovative ways Ada as a community has used partnerships between key stakeholders to create first class opportunities for our students and residents alike.

Air Evac

Air Evac recently expanded and added a helicopter facility at the Ada Municipal Airport. Proposition 2 funds helped incentivize Air Evac's expansion into Ada. This expansion allows our health care providers to increase services, particularly during emergencies.

Oka' Institute

Prop 2 funds matched contributions from the Chickasaw Nation and ECU Foundation in order to launch an institute focused on water research, policy, and technology. This center is unique to the state and the region, and will help Ada's efforts to further develop economic development based around innovation and water-related research.

Pontotoc Technology Center Business Incubator

Lovera's is a national award winning cheese maker who are currently producing new cheeses made from local ingredients at the Pontotoc Technology Center Business Incubator. The Tech Center's incubator facility needed a new floor in order for Lovera's to meet FDA regulations for food production. Proposition 2 partially funded a new floor at the Tech center so that the incubator can keep hosting Lovera's and other new businesses in the future.

iQor

Prop 2 funds provided an employment incentive for the call center operation. iQor is currently using a building which was originally constructed for Sykes, another Prop 2 project mentioned below..

Stone Creek Development

Stone Creek is a new retail development. Prop 2 funds helped pay for the construction a new lift station so that local infrastructure can meet the increased demand from new retail developments in the area.

Superior Fabrications

Superior Fabrications expanded into a former Remington plant. Prop 2 funds helped secure the former plant and facilitate the land needed for Superior's expansion.

IRT

Proposition 2 funds helped offset the transition and relocation costs which allowed IRT to expand into the Ada area.

True Steel

True Steel relocated in Ada to 2007. Prop 2 funds helped provide incentives for adding quality jobs to the workforce. Since the incentive went into effect, True Steel has added employment and expanded their operation.

Council on Law Enforcement Education and Training Facility

Proposition 2 helped fund the construction of the CLEET facility. The facility was awarded to Ada through a competitive, statewide bidding process in 2005. The facility employees approximately 40 people and brings in over 500 law enforcement cadets each year. These cadets and employees patronize Ada's businesses during each course.

Globe Manufacturing

Prop 2 funds allowed the Ada Jobs Foundation and Ada Industrial Development Corporation to purchase a former Wrangler plant in 2004. This plant was sold to Globe Manufacturing who are one of the leading producers of turndown suits used by firefighters throughout the country.

GAMI/Tornado Alley

In 2002, Proposition 2 funds allowed GAMI and Tornado Alley to expand their operations at the Ada Airport. GAMI an innovative and leading company which produces aftermarket products for piston aircraft engines.

Sykes Building

Proposition 2 funded the construction of a building for Sykes Enterprises in 2000. The building has hosted IRT and is currently being used by iQor as a large call center.

Outcomes and Data

Ada's economy has fared well since Proposition 2 went into effect in 1998. This section compares economic data before and after Proposition 2 went into effect. Over a period of 18 years, employment is up 35%, the average wage for the county has nearly doubled and more than outpaced inflation. Sales tax deposits have increased by 64% which provides additional evidence that people in Ada are spending more with increased incomes. This data also shows that Ada has done well in weathering two national recessions as well as the current drop in oil prices which have affected the region. Compared to conditions before Proposition 2, Ada's economy has measured well since 1998 and has shown strong signs of growth.

The number of people who are employed in Ada has increased significantly since Proposition 2 first went into effect in 1998. Using data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the number of people employed in Ada grew 35% from 1998 to 2016. This is an increase of 4,590 people, or an average of 255 new jobs a year since Proposition 2 has gone into effect. This increase is significant compared to the decade before Proposition 2â€™s effect, and approximately 88% of manufacturing industry workers in the Ada area work for a company impacted by Proposition 2 funds.

Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Local Area Unemployment Statistics for the Ada, OK Micropolitan Area

Wages have followed a similar pattern. Using inflation adjusted data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics Quarterly Census on Employment and Wages (QCEW) from 1990, the adjusted average annual wage for Pontotoc County has grown by 30 percent from 1998 to 2016. When adjusted for inflation, the value of the average wage in Pontotoc County from 1990 to 1998 appear to have been stagnant. Since Proposition 2 has gone into effect, the average wage has grown significantly.

Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages for Pontotoc County. All dollar values are adjusted to 2016 values using Consumer Price Index (CPI) figures.

While wages and employment have increased, there is also evidence for an increasing level of retail sales activity for Ada. According to data from the Oklahoma Tax Commission, total annual sales tax deposits for Ada have increased by $5,917,339 from 1998 to 2016. This represents an increase of 64% for sales tax deposits, and equates to an additional $150 million in retail sales in Ada.

Source: Oklahoma Tax Commission, Sales Tax Collections by NAICS for Ada, Oklahoma

Proposition 2 is just one part of Ada's economic success. Ada's economy received a major "boost" around 2005. This may be caused, in part, by the growing success of the Chickasaw Nation. Legal Shield and East Central University were also expanding during this period as well. While there is no way to prove what would have happened without Propoistion 2, this data shows Ada have have had nearly two decades of sustained economic success, and efforts funded by Proposition 2, such as retailing Legal Shield and Flex-N-Gate, appear to have played a role in this success.

Proposition 2 has been used in innovative ways, particularly for a community the size of Ada. While many communities use funds to attract new companies, Proposition 2 dollars have been used to help retain major employers, repurpose empty manufacturing buildings, and invest in local educational facilities. The funds have also gone to help a diverse array of companies, industries and projects. These unique aspects to Proposition 2 may have helped Ada better weather state and national recessions. As economic development efforts move forward in Ada, innovation and diversity appear to be key to future success.
